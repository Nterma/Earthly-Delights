🥒 Product Care & Safety

How should I prepare my produce before use? ▼
Always wash thoroughly with warm water and mild soap before use. Some people like to gently dry with a paper towel. For internal use, always use a premium condom to prevent irritation or infection. Never use sharp utensils that might create splinters.
Can I eat the produce after use? ▼
No. Once used for intimate purposes, the produce should be discarded. Even with thorough cleaning, it's best to compost it after use. Your health and safety are our priority.
How long does produce last? ▼
Fresh produce typically lasts 3-7 days in a standard refrigerator, depending on the variety. Some items (like carrots) last longer; others (like peaches) are best used within 2-3 days. Use your sense of smell and touch—if it smells off or feels squishy, it's time to compost.
Is it safe for sensitive skin? ▼
Organic produce is chemical-free, which makes it gentle. However, always use a condom for internal use. If you have known allergies to specific produce, avoid that item. When in doubt, test on a non-sensitive area first.
Should I use lubricant? ▼
Yes, we recommend a water-based lubricant for added comfort and safety. This reduces friction and enhances the experience. Always ensure any lubricant is condom-safe if using protection.

❄️ Storage & Temperature Play

Can I use cold produce for temperature play? ▼
Absolutely! Many customers enjoy the sensation of chilled produce. Store your item in the refrigerator for 20-30 minutes before use. For an even cooler experience, place it in the freezer for 5-10 minutes (but not longer, as extreme cold can be uncomfortable).
What about warm produce? ▼
You can leave produce at room temperature for a different sensation. Never use a microwave or oven, as this can create hot spots and cause burns. Room temperature produce is safe and pleasant.
